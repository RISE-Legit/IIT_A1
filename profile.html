Cyber Security Operations Analyst
Hays Information Technology

Job Description

The job is for a security analyst who will provide ongoing support for a client in the digital healthcare sector. Their duties will include security event management (threat response, analysis and prevention) for vendors. This is appealing to me for the fact that this is an ever-evolving landscape. As the threats become more advanced, the response must also evolve. This opens the door for creative problem solving. In saying that, there is still a place for the low-level security prevention methods. I feel this would also give me great experience for taking this career further, perhaps working with the government or on more of a 'cyber frontline', if you will.

The experience required by this job would include a number of necessary skills. First of all a working knowledge of the Protective Security Policy Framework (PSPF) (Protectivesecurity.gov.au, 2019) created by the Australian Government to protect the people, information and assets of government entities both locally and abroad. Alongside the PSPF, knowledge of the Information Security Manual (ISM) (Cyber.gov.au, 2019), created by the Australian Signals Directorate would be necessary. This is a cyber security framework designed to protect the information systems of organisations.

An operation analyst would also need network operational knowledge as they will be checking the organisations networks for security shortfalls as well as monitoring for breaches and responding accordingly. Their would be a lot of paperwork as well, as contingency plans will need to be drafted if there ever was a breach. Educating the staff in the organisation you work for is also an iportant part of this role, as a castle's walls can be easily undermined if a door is left open.

Am I currently a good fit for this job? Not even remotely. Despite listening to a number of cyber security related podcasts and having a keen interest in this field, I have no practical experience and feel I would fall flat of a security situation were to arise. While I may know that the USA is poking around in networks in Russia, I wouldn't be able to close a port on a firewall to stop the worlds most inept hacker.

To get myself to the skill level required by this position, I would need a number of years experience working in a security capacity. Whether that be as a network security operator or something similar to give me the basic skills to protect myself. I would aslo need some degree of penetration testing skills to know what I should be looking for when people do attack the network. I feel that would also give me some great technical ability. I would need to be submerged in the security operations field so as to learn the best practices and methods by which outcomes are achieved and security stability garnered.

I would also need to be familiar with the government policy, namely the PSPF and ISM, but also be aware of international policy like NIST, National Cyber Security and European Union Agency for Network and Information Security. These policies may vary on the specifics from country to country, but they do have a common goal, so being aware of their differences may prove beneficial in countering security threats as well as bolstering out one's own efforts.
